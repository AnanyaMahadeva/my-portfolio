Project Overview

This project applies a statistical and machine learning pipeline to detect fraudulent financial transactions within a dataset of 500 customer records. Using Z-score standardisation, IQR outlier detection, correlation analysis, and a Random Forest classifier, the analysis identifies the behavioural and demographic patterns most strongly associated with fraud.

Dataset: 500 customer transaction records with 24 variables — demographic, financial, and behavioural.

Target Variable: is_fraud (binary) — 246 fraud cases (49.2%) and 254 non-fraud (50.8%).

Model: Random Forest Classifier — 67.2% accuracy, 70% recall on fraud class.

import pandas as pd

# Simulate dataset matching the report's descriptive statistics exactly

    "is_fraud": fraud_label,
    "age": np.random.normal(50.1, 14.4, n).clip(18, 90),
    "personal_income": np.where(
        fraud_label == 1,
        np.random.normal(58000, 18000, n),
        np.random.normal(65000, 20000, n)
    ).clip(15000, 122755),
    "family_income": np.random.normal(90508, 23825, n).clip(19922, 157695),
    "credit_score": np.where(

        np.random.normal(665, 80, n)

    "transaction_amount": np.random.exponential(2000, n).clip(10, 15050),
    "loan_amount": np.random.exponential(19889, n).clip(4, 115848),

Correlation of numerical features with the fraud label, based on report findings.

Interpretation: Most variables show weak correlations with fraud (|r| < 0.3), confirming that fraud patterns are complex and multidimensional. No single predictor is sufficient — a multivariate machine learning approach is essential.

  • fraud_amount (r = 0.538): strongest signal, but post-fraud indicator
  • credit_score (r = −0.234): lower scores correlate with higher fraud risk
  • personal_income (r = −0.207): lower income customers show higher vulnerability

Key Insights

Finding Implication
Female customers show higher fraud vulnerability (55.17%) Gender-aware verification strategies warranted
Desktop transactions carry highest fraud risk (54.81%) Strengthen multi-factor authentication on desktop
Most fraud involves amounts under €1,000 Micro-fraud monitoring is as critical as high-value alerts
Fraud rate highest in West (54.76%) and East (50.38%) regions Regional resource prioritisation needed
High-value fraud occurs with low transaction frequency Flag large single transactions from low-activity accounts

Recommendations

Six evidence-based recommendations from the analysis:

  1. Apply extra verification for customers with lower incomes, lower credit scores, and those aged 50+
  2. Strengthen desktop and in-store authentication channels where fraud rates are highest
  3. Monitor small transactions — most fraud occurs under €1,000 and can precede larger attacks
  4. Deploy real-time alerts to notify customers of unusual activity immediately
  5. Combine Z-score and IQR statistical methods with Random Forest for layered detection
  6. Prioritise fraud prevention resources in West and East regions
